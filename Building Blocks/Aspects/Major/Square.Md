# Square

## Overview
Squares occur at 90° and are engines of friction. They force movement, expose internal conflict, and catalyze breakthroughs when the pressure is used constructively rather than resisted.

## Key Traits
- Creates crises that require decisive action.
- Reveals blind spots between incompatible modalities or elements.
- Rewards discipline, resilience, and conscious problem-solving.

## Examples
- A Saturn–Uranus square often describes generational upheaval that restructures societal systems.
**Natal Chart Example.** Someone with Mercury square Pluto may wrestle with intense thoughts that surface abruptly. By committing to investigative journalism or therapeutic writing practices, they learn to process deep material responsibly, turning the tension into incisive communication.
**Transit Example.** When Jupiter transits square a natal Moon, habitual comfort zones are challenged. The person might accept an invitation to live abroad or expand their family support network, stretching emotional coping skills while discovering new sources of generosity.
**Synastry Example.** In partnership, one person’s Sun square the other’s Mars can ignite productive rivalry. They may motivate each other through shared fitness goals or collaborative projects where deadlines spur both to sharpen strategy and endurance.

## Use In Practice
Squares respond to planned outlets—goal-setting, coaching, and structured accountability keep the energy moving. Treat friction as a training ground rather than a sign of failure.
