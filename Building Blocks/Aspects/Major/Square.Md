# Square

## Overview
Squares occur at 90° and are engines of friction. They force movement, expose internal conflict, and catalyze breakthroughs when the pressure is used constructively rather than resisted.

## Key Traits
- Creates crises that require decisive action.
- Reveals blind spots between incompatible modalities or elements.
- Rewards discipline, resilience, and conscious problem-solving.

## Example Interpretations
1. **Natal Chart Example**
   In a natal chart, a square describes an internal crossroads where competing drives demand constructive outlets. The individual learns resilience by turning friction into disciplined practice, structured problem-solving, and courageous self-confrontation that forges strength over time.
2. **Transit Example**
   Transit squares press for decisive change. Circumstances expose tensions that can no longer be postponed, prompting strategic planning, boundary work, or skill upgrades. Meeting the challenge with accountability converts pressure into momentum.
3. **Synastry Example**
   Between charts, a square fuels dynamic growth through contrast. The relationship thrives when both parties channel the spark into shared projects, clear agreements, and regular check-ins that keep competition friendly while honoring individual limits.
4. **Progressed Chart Example**
   A progressed square marks a developmental training ground. Emerging needs clash with established habits, encouraging the native to redesign routines, embrace mentorship, and commit to disciplined practice that crystallizes a more resilient identity.

## Use In Practice
Squares respond to planned outlets—goal-setting, coaching, and structured accountability keep the energy moving. Treat friction as a training ground rather than a sign of failure.
